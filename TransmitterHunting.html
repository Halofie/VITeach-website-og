Transmitter Hunting

What is Transmitter Hunting?

Transmitter hunting, also known as fox hunting, is a radio sport where participants use direction-finding techniques to locate hidden transmitters, or "foxes".

Transmitter Hunting in a Nutshell:

  • Setup:

    Craft a challenge by hiding a transmitter with specific settings.

  • Gear:

    Armed with handheld RDF equipment, use antennas and receivers, some with personalized directional gear.

  • Tracking:

    Listen for the signal, interpret strength and direction to pinpoint the hidden transmitter.

  • Navigation & Strategy:

    Success lies in a blend of navigation skills and strategic thinking. Hunters collaborate, triangulating the transmitter's position and adjusting their course based on signal strength changes.

Variations of Transmitter Hunting:

  • Mobile Hunt Adventure:

    Explore with RDF-equipped vehicles for a dynamic chase!

  • Amateur Radio Socials:

    Join transmitter hunts for camaraderie and radio fun with clubs!

  • Fox Hunt Competition:

    Turn up the heat! Compete to find hidden transmitters in record time.

  • Rescue Ready:
